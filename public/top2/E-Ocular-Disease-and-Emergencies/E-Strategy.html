⚡ Fast Picks — exam shortcuts
Read the options first, kill wrong values, then settle the flip. cue → rule → trap.
Paired opposites — cue: tender/chronic, in/out, mild/severe.
  • ›Stye = tender/acute; chalazion = non-tender/chronic. Entropion = turns in (lashes scratch); ectropion = turns out (CN7). Episcleritis = mild; scleritis = boring pain.
  • ✗Trap: ectropion "scratches the cornea" — that's entropion.
Signature fundus → diagnosis — cue: the described picture.
  • ›Cherry-red = CRAO; blood-and-thunder = CRVO; bone spicules = RP; macular star = HTN III.
  • ✗Trap: "cherry-red spot is diseased tissue" — it's the spared choroid-fed fovea.
Glaucoma type — cue: open vs closed angle.
  • ›Open = silent, medical; closed = acute emergency, laser iridotomy. Phacolytic = open; phacomorphic = closed.
  • ✗Trap: treating angle-closure medically first / calling phacolytic "closed-angle."
"Earliest" vs "most common/classic" — cue: the word "earliest".
  • ›Earliest DR sign = microaneurysm; earliest keratoconus = Fleischer ring; earliest chloroquine = ↓VA/color (bull's-eye is late).
  • ✗Trap: picking the classic late sign when the stem says "earliest."
PH blindness vs low vision — cue: which one the stem asks.
  • ›Blindness → cataract leads. Low vision → refractive error leads.
  • ✗Trap: using the blindness order for a low-vision question.

E1Orbit, Adnexa & Eyelids

Paired opposites: Hordeolum (acute, tender abscess) vs Chalazion (chronic, non-tender granuloma) · Entropion (in, lashes scratch) vs Ectropion (out, CN VII).
Key associations: Buphthalmos = congenital glaucoma · Orbital cellulitis = ethmoiditis · Horner's = ptosis + miosis + anhidrosis · Most common lid malignancy = basal cell carcinoma.
Lacrimal: chronic dacryocystitis → DCR; blood from the sac = tumor (SCC); malignant lacrimal gland tumor = adenoid cystic carcinoma; Mikulicz = lacrimal + salivary enlargement.
Proptosis: thyroid (Graves) is most common overall; pulsating = carotid-cavernous fistula; pediatric orbital malignancy = rhabdomyosarcoma.
Trap: Horner's causes a dilated pupil. No — miosis (small). The dilated-pupil ptosis is CN III palsy.
Trap: ectropion scratches the cornea. No — that is entropion; ectropion turns out and causes exposure.

E2Anterior Segment

Pterygium crosses the limbus and destroys Bowman's; pinguecula does not. Ciliary (circumcorneal) flush = deep danger (uveitis, keratitis, acute glaucoma).
Cornea clues: Fleischer ring = earliest keratoconus · Kayser-Fleischer = copper in Descemet's (Wilson's) · Krukenberg spindle = pigment dispersion · dendritic ulcer = herpes (never steroids on epithelial disease).
Glaucoma: POAG = silent, open, medical; PACG = emergency, closed, mid-dilated fixed pupil, surgical. Phacolytic = open (leaked protein); phacomorphic = closed (swollen lens).
Cataract/lens: nuclear = second sight (myopic shift) · PSC = most disabling, steroid-linked · Marfan up, homocystinuria down · aphakia ≈ +10 to +12 D.
Uveitis: mutton-fat KP = granulomatous; hypopyon = HLA-B27/Behcet's; treat anterior uveitis with topical steroid + cycloplegic. Dry eye: Schirmer <10 mm, TBUT <10 s.
Trap: Kayser-Fleischer is iron. No — it is copper in Descemet's (Wilson's).
Trap: phacolytic glaucoma is closed-angle. No — it is open-angle; phacomorphic is closed.

E3Posterior Segment & Retina

Signature pictures: cherry-red = CRAO · blood-and-thunder = CRVO · bone spicules + nyctalopia = RP · macular star + papilledema = malignant HTN · headlight in fog = toxoplasmosis · pizza pie = CMV.
Diabetic ladder: microaneurysm (earliest) → cotton-wool/IRMA → new vessels. Severe NPDR = 4-2-1; PDR → panretinal photocoagulation. Cotton-wool spots = NFL infarcts, not exudates.
Retinal detachment: only rhegmatogenous has a break (horseshoe tear, tobacco dust); tractional and exudative do not. ARMD: dry 85–90%; wet → intravitreal anti-VEGF.
Optic nerveKey feature
Optic neuritisUnilateral, painful, RAPD, MS

Trap: the cherry-red spot is diseased tissue. No — it is the healthy choroid-fed fovea showing through pale ischemic retina.
Trap: papilledema causes early vision loss. No — vision is spared early; optic neuritis loses vision early.

Chemicals: alkali > acid (liquefactive vs coagulative); hydrofluoric acid and ammonia penetrate deeply. Prognosis follows limbal ischemia (Roper-Hall).
Trauma: ferrous IOFB = no MRI (use CT); iron = siderosis, copper alloy = chalcosis. Hyphema rebleeds days 3–5. Shaken baby = bilateral retinal hemorrhages.
Trap: take a history before treating a chemical burn. No — irrigate first; it is the only injury treated before history.
Trap: acute angle-closure has a small constricted pupil. No — it is mid-dilated and fixed.
Trap: patch a ruptured globe. No — shield it with no pressure.
