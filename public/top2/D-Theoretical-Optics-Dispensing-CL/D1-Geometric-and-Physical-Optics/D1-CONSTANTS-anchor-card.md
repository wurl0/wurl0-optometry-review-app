## Nodal Points and Center of Rotation

All measured from the anterior cornea.

| Point | Location |
|---|---|
| Primary nodal point (N) | 7.08mm |
| Secondary nodal point (N') | 7.33mm |
| Center of rotation | 13.5mm |

Nodal points are used for retinal image size and visual axis calculations.
Center of rotation is the pivot point for all eye movements — different measurement, easy to confuse with nodal point.

## Why the Posterior Cornea is Minus

Two reasons:
1. n drops from cornea (1.376) to aqueous (1.336) — going from denser to less dense
2. The surface is convex toward the incoming light

When n decreases at a convex surface, the surface diverges light instead of converging it — that makes it minus power. The posterior cornea works against the anterior surface, which is why net corneal power (~43D) is less than what the front surface alone would produce (~48D).

## Focal Lengths

All measured from the anterior corneal apex.

| Model | Primary f (in front of eye) | Secondary f' (at retina) |
|---|---|---|
| Emsley reduced eye | 16.67mm | 22.22mm |
| Gullstrand exact eye | 15.70mm | 24.38mm |

Primary focal length = where an object must sit so light exits the eye as parallel rays.
Secondary focal length = where parallel incoming light focuses (at the retina in emmetropia).

**Why 24mm ≠ 24.38mm:**
24mm is the empirically measured average axial length of a real emmetropic eye.
24.38mm is where Gullstrand's mathematical model places F'. The model is a mathematical idealization — not a biological average. The real eye is slightly shorter than the model predicts.

## Axes and Lines

Each line is defined by which point it passes through.

| Line/Axis | Passes through | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Optic axis | Centers of curvature of all refracting surfaces | Geometric centerline — no fixation point involved |
| Visual axis | Nodal point | From fixation point → nodal point → fovea |
| Line of sight | Center of entrance pupil | From fixation point → entrance pupil center → fovea |
| Pupillary axis | Center of entrance pupil, perpendicular to cornea | Reference axis — does not go to the fovea |

**Fixation point** = the point in space the eye is looking at.

---

## Angles

| Angle | Between | Measured at |
|---|---|---|
| Alpha (α) | Visual axis and optic axis | Nodal point |
| Kappa (κ) | Visual axis and pupillary axis | Corneal plane |
| Gamma (γ) | Line of sight and optic axis | Center of rotation |
| Lambda (λ) | Pupillary axis and line of sight | — |

Normal angle alpha = positive (fovea is temporal to posterior pole; visual axis tilts slightly relative to optic axis).
Angle kappa is used clinically — a large positive kappa mimics exotropia (pseudo-exotropia).

## Principal Planes

| Plane | Location from corneal apex |
|---|---|
| H (anterior principal plane) | 1.35mm behind cornea |
| H' (posterior principal plane) | 1.60mm behind cornea |

Both planes sit close to the cornea. Focal lengths and image distances in Gullstrand's model are measured from H and H', not from the corneal apex — which is why Gullstrand's focal lengths differ slightly from the reduced eye values.
