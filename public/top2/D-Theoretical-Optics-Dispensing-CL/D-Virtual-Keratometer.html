How the B&L one-position mires behave
  • Focus and centre on the large + first. The central mire carries a big plus cross. Off focus the mires blur. Sharpen and centre on that cross before touching a drum, or every later reading inherits the error.
  • One mire, doubled into three images. You see the central image plus a left copy and an upper copy. Turn the horizontal drum until the + signs of the central and left images superimpose; turn the vertical drum until the − signs of the central and upper images superimpose. When both are nulled the copies fall back onto the centre and the image is no longer doubled. Each drum reads dioptres of corneal power directly.
  • Set the axis before the drums. Off a principal meridian the plus signs step apart vertically (the mires look broken). Rotate the wheel until they run unbroken, then null. A principal meridian is found at the axis and again 90° away.
  • Two meridians, at right angles. The flatter power sits on one, the steeper on the one 90° from it. Their difference is the corneal astigmatism.
  • With-the-rule = steeper meridian vertical (near 90). Against-the-rule = steeper horizontal (near 180). Anything in between is oblique.

Dioptres to millimetres: r = 337.5 / K, using the keratometric index 1.3375. The drum is graded in dioptres; the millimetre radius follows from that one constant.

Javal's rule

Javal's rule, part by part

Atotal = 1.25 × Acorneal + 0.50 D against-the-rule

  • Acorneal is the astigmatism you just measured: the difference between the two Ks, its axis the steep meridian.
  • × 1.25 because the crystalline lens and the posterior cornea add a little more cylinder in the same sense, so the eye's total runs a shade larger than the front surface alone.
  • + 0.50 D against-the-rule is the physiological lenticular astigmatism nearly every eye carries. It subtracts from with-the-rule corneal astigmatism and adds to against-the-rule.

Worked both ways: 2.00 D WTR cornea → 1.25 × 2.00 = 2.50, minus 0.50 ATR → 2.00 D WTR total. 1.00 D ATR cornea → 1.25 × 1.00 = 1.25, plus 0.50 ATR → 1.75 D ATR total.

This is the simplified scalar form used in most reviewers. The full rule is a vector sum; the numbers agree when the corneal axis is close to 90 or 180, which is the case being drilled here.
