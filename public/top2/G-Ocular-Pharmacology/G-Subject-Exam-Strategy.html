⚡ Fast Picks — exam shortcuts
Read the options first, kill wrong values, then settle the flip. cue → rule → trap.
Autonomic pupil effect — cue: agonist/antagonist + system.
  • ›Number rule: 1 constricts, 2 dilates (α1 mydriasis, α2 cuts aqueous, β2 raises aqueous). Dilate w/o cycloplegia = sympathomimetic; dilate + cycloplegia = parasympatholytic; constrict = parasympathomimetic.
  • ✗Trap: "β2 stimulation lowers IOP" — β2 increases aqueous.
Cycloplegic strength — cue: strongest/longest vs routine.
  • ›Atropine = strongest/longest (14 d); tropicamide = weakest/shortest; cyclopentolate = routine DOC.
  • ✗Trap: "tropicamide is the strongest cycloplegic."
Glaucoma drug mechanism — cue: cut production vs boost outflow.
  • ›Cut production (3): β-blockers, α2 agonists, CAIs. Boost outflow (2): prostaglandins (uveoscleral), miotics (trabecular).
  • ✗Trap: "β-blockers increase outflow" or "prostaglandins drain trabecular."
Pupil-test pairs — cue: cocaine / hydroxyamphetamine / pilocarpine.
  • ›Cocaine confirms Horner (fails to dilate); hydroxyamphetamine localizes; 0.1% pilocarpine constricts an Adie's pupil.
  • ✗Trap: a Horner pupil "dilates with cocaine" — it fails to.
Drug → ocular sign — cue: the drug named.
  • ›Chloroquine = bull's-eye (late); digitalis = xanthopsia (yellow); ethambutol/INH = optic neuritis + red-green; steroid = PSC cataract + glaucoma.
  • ✗Trap: "ethambutol = blue-yellow defect" — it's red-green.

G1General Pharmacology Principles

Two buckets: Kinetics = what the body does to the drug (ADME). Dynamics = what the drug does to the body (receptor + effect).
Core rules: liver metabolizes (P-450), kidney excretes. Lipid = uncharged = absorbable. k = 0.693/t½ (0.693 = ln 2). Vd = most important parameter. TI = LD50/ED50.
Combos: Additive 1+1=2 · Synergism 1+1>2 · Potentiation 0+1>1. Bonds: covalent = strongest (irreversible); Van der Waals = weakest, most common (reversible = competitive antagonism).
Misc: potency = low dose, efficacy = max effect. IV = 100% bioavailable. Atropine excreted unchanged in urine. Schedule I = no medical use. Rx = recipe ('to take').
Trap: potency equals efficacy. No — potency is the dose needed; efficacy is the maximum effect.

G2Autonomic Drugs

2x2 grid: Sphincter = M3 (parasympathetic, miosis); dilator = α1 (sympathetic, mydriasis). Number rule: 1 constricts, 2 dilates (α1 mydriasis, α2 cuts aqueous, β2 raises aqueous).
CycloplegicDuration

Cyclopentolate (routine cycloplegic)6–24 h

Pupil tests: Adie's constricts to 0.1% pilocarpine; Horner's: cocaine confirms (fails to dilate), hydroxyamphetamine localizes. Echothiopate/DFP = iris cysts (reverse with 2.5% phenylephrine). Organophosphate poisoning antidote = atropine.
Suffixes: -olol = beta blocker · -onidine = α2 agonist · -zoline = α1 decongestant · -stigmine = AChE inhibitor.
Trap: tropicamide is the strongest cycloplegic. No — it is the weakest/shortest; atropine is strongest.

G3Diagnostic Agents (Anesthetics & Dyes)

Anesthetics block Na⁺ channels. Count the i's: one = ester (cocaine, proparacaine, tetracaine; plasma cholinesterase), two = amide (lidocaine; liver). Proparacaine = everyday drop; cocaine diagnoses Horner's but NOT for tonometry (softens cornea).
Dye sorting: Fluorescein = the living gap (epithelial defects, cobalt blue 465 nm) · Rose bengal / lissamine = the dead cells (use rose bengal before fluorescein) · Trypan / ICG = the endothelium/choroid.
Tests: Seidel = aqueous wound leak; Jones I/II = lacrimal drainage. Add epinephrine to prolong a block, bicarbonate to speed it.
Trap: anesthetics work better in infected tissue. No — acidic tissue ionizes the drug and weakens the block.

G4Therapeutic Agents

Glaucoma "3 down, 2 out": cut production = Beta blockers, Alpha-2 agonists, CAIs. Boost outflow = Prostaglandins (uveoscleral, first-line), Miotics (trabecular).
Watch-outs: acetazolamide (CAI) = sulfa allergy + paresthesia; betaxolol is the asthma-safe beta blocker; mannitol IV for acute angle-closure. Steroids inhibit phospholipase A2 → ocular S/E = glaucoma + posterior subcapsular cataract; fluorometholone is safest for IOP.
Antibiotic pearls: tetracycline = trachoma DOC; aminoglycosides = oto/nephrotoxic >5 days; chloramphenicol = aplastic anemia; ethambutol/INH = optic neuritis; natamycin = filamentary fungi.
Trap: prostaglandins drain through the trabeculum. No — they open the uveoscleral route.
Trap: acetazolamide is safe in sulfa allergy. No — it is a sulfonamide; avoid it.
