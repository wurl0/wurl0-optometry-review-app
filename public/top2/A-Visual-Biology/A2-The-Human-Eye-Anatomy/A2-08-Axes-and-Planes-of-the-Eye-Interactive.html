Axes of the Eye (Axes of Fick)

Three imaginary lines, all passing through the center of rotation. The eye spins around an axis, and the axis itself stays put. Tap an axis to watch the movement it produces. The white notch on the cornea is your reference point.

Why the movement is perpendicular to the axis: a door swings across the room, but its hinge runs straight up. The eye does the same. Planes of the Eye

A plane is built from two axes, and it slices the globe into two halves. Tap a plane to drop it into the same eye. Notice it always lies flat across the two axes it contains.
