Direction of gaze (6 cardinal positions)

The agonist in each eye sits at the corner that points toward the gaze. Up = top row, down = bottom row, the obliques (IO/SO) handle up-in and down-in, the vertical recti (SR/IR) handle up-out and down-out.

The two laws behind the ring

Hering's law (both eyes, equal innervation): the impulse to an agonist goes equally to its yoke muscle in the other eye, so the eyes stay parallel. That yoke pair is exactly what lights up when you pick a gaze. Example: look right → R-LR + L-MR fire together.
Sherrington's law (one eye, reciprocal innervation): when a muscle contracts, its direct antagonist relaxes by the same amount. That is the red muscle in each eye. Example: supraduction → SR contracts (+++), IR relaxes (−−−).

So every gaze you tap shows both laws at once: Hering across the two eyes (the green pair), Sherrington inside each eye (green agonist, red antagonist).
