Tap any station. Afferent carries light in; the reflex branches off at the pretectum and feeds both Edinger-Westphal nuclei (that's why light in one eye constricts both pupils). The parasympathetic efferent constricts; the 3-neuron sympathetic chain dilates.

Consensual response: each pretectal nucleus projects to both Edinger-Westphal nuclei, so light in ONE eye constricts BOTH pupils (direct + consensual). The reflex leaves the visual pathway before the LGN/cortex — so cortical lesions never change the pupil.
