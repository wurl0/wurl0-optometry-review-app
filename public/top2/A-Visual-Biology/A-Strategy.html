⚡ Fast Picks — exam shortcuts
Read the options first, kill wrong values, then settle the flip. cue → rule → trap.
CN3 palsy — cue: a third-nerve palsy + pupil status.
  • ›Pupil dilated → compressive (aneurysm) = emergency, image now. Pupil spared → ischemic (diabetic) = monitor.
  • ✗Trap: sending every CN3 palsy to surgery; the pupil-spared one is observed.
EOM innervation — cue: a named muscle.
  • ›LR6 SO4 AO3: Lateral Rectus → CN6, Superior Oblique → CN4, All Others → CN3.
  • ✗Trap: the option saying "CN3 supplies all the extraocular muscles."
Field defect by lesion site — cue: where the lesion is.
  • ›Chiasm → bitemporal; behind chiasm → homonymous (more congruous the more posterior; occipital = most congruous + macular sparing).
  • ✗Trap: calling an occipital defect "incongruous" — incongruous = optic tract.
Germ layer — cue: which structure.
  • ›Surface ectoderm = lens + corneal epithelium. Neuroectoderm = retina, optic nerve, sphincter/dilator. Neural crest = corneal stroma/endothelium, sclera, uvea.
  • ✗Trap: labeling corneal endothelium "mesoderm" — it is neural crest.

A1Body Systems & Cranial Nerves

EOM innervation "LR6 SO4 AO3": Lateral Rectus = CN6, Superior Oblique = CN4, All Others = CN3.
CN3 pupil rule: Compressive (aneurysm) blows the pupil (outer pupillomotor fibers hit first) = emergency. Ischemic (diabetic) spares the pupil.
CN4 is special: only fully decussated nerve (contralateral SO), thinnest motor nerve, longest intracranial course (~75 mm).
Sinus rule: Ethmoid → orbital cellulitis (thin lamina papyracea). Sphenoid → retrobulbar neuritis (CN2 sits above).
Trigeminal: V1 (ophthalmic) = smallest, pure sensory, gives corneal sensation (nasociliary). V3 = largest. CN7 = lid closure + reflex tears.
Trap: CN4 supplies the ipsilateral SO. No — it crosses to the contralateral SO.
Trap: a pupil-involving CN3 palsy can be observed. No — rule out aneurysm immediately.

A2Orbit & Extraocular Muscles

SO primary action = intorsion (depression secondary). IO primary = extorsion. IO is the only EOM not arising from the annulus of Zinn (it starts at the maxilla).
Walls: medial wall thinnest (lamina papyracea); lateral wall thickest. CN2 → optic canal; CN3/4/6 + V1 → superior orbital fissure.
Spiral of Tillaux: MR inserts nearest the limbus (5.5 mm, strongest); SR farthest (7.7 mm). Recti insert in front of the equator, obliques behind. SO is the longest EOM (~60 mm).
Evisceration = remove contents, keep sclera. Enucleation = remove the whole globe. Lockwood's ligament = suspensory hammock.
Trap: the SO elevates the eye. No — primary action is intorsion (IO elevates, SO depresses).
Trap: CN2 runs through the SOF. No — CN2 goes through the optic canal.

A2Eyelids, Conjunctiva & Lacrimal

Tear film (outside → cornea): Lipid → Aqueous → Mucin. Aqueous is thickest (~7 µm). Lipid = Meibomian; basic aqueous = Krause + Wolfring; reflex aqueous = main gland (CN7); mucin = goblet cells.
Tests: Schirmer = aqueous volume (10–25 mm/5 min); TBUT = stability (>10 s). Normal Schirmer + short TBUT = evaporative dry eye (MGD).
Ptosis: Müller's paralysis = mild 1–2 mm (Horner's); LPS paralysis = complete (CN3). NLD drains to the inferior meatus (valve of Hasner).
Tears: major Ig = secretory IgA. Goblet cells absent at lid margins and limbus. Crocodile tears = misdirected CN7 fibers.
Trap: the aqueous layer is thinnest. No — aqueous is the thickest layer.
Trap: Schirmer measures stability. No — Schirmer = volume; TBUT = stability.

A2Cornea & Sclera

6 layers: Epithelium → Bowman's → Stroma → Dua's → Descemet's → Endothelium. Stroma = 90% of thickness. Cornea power ~43 D (strongest refractor).
Regeneration: epithelium and Descemet's regenerate; Bowman's and endothelium do NOT (endothelium is post-mitotic). Fuchs' = endothelial loss → pump fails → edema + guttata.
Clarity: endothelial Na-K ATPase keeps the stroma deturgesced. Regular collagen = clear cornea; irregular collagen = white sclera. Cornea is thinner centrally and oval (wider horizontally).
Sclera: thinnest at EOM insertion (0.3 mm), thickest at CN2 (1.0 mm). Nasociliary innervates all corneal layers except Descemet's and endothelium.
Trap: the cornea is thickest centrally. No — it is thinner centrally, thicker peripherally.
Trap: endothelium regenerates. No — post-mitotic; lost cells are gone permanently.

A2Uveal Tract

Uvea = Iris + Ciliary body + Choroid. Sphincter (miosis) = parasympathetic CN3; dilator (mydriasis) = sympathetic.
Accommodation: ciliary muscle contracts → zonules relax → lens rounds up. Pars plana = safe surgical entry (least vascular); pars plicata makes aqueous.
Pupils: Argyll-Robertson (small, irregular, light-near dissociation) = neurosyphilis. Adie's (tonic) = ciliary ganglion, benign, constricts to 0.125% pilocarpine (supersensitivity).
Choroid thickest at the posterior pole; choriocapillaris feeds the outer retina; Bruch's membrane sits between choroid and RPE.
Trap: ciliary contraction tightens the zonules. No — it relaxes them; the lens rounds by elastic recoil.
Trap: Argyll-Robertson is benign. No — that is Adie's; Argyll-Robertson means neurosyphilis.

A2Lens, Vitreous & Aqueous

Aqueous: made by the non-pigment epithelium of the pars plicata (~2 µL/min). Outflow 80% trabecular, 20% uveoscleral (prostaglandins boost uveoscleral). IOP 10–21 mmHg.
Capsule: anterior capsule is thickest (~15 µm, the thickest basement membrane in the body); posterior is thinnest (~2.2 µm). Posterior lens surface is more curved (steeper).
Presbyopia = lens nuclear hardening, not ciliary muscle weakness. Vitreous ~98.5% water (hyaluronic acid gel), not replaced if lost. Cloquet's canal = hyaloid remnant.
Aqueous has higher ascorbate than plasma and very low protein. The lens is avascular to stay optically clear.
Trap: uveoscleral is 80% of outflow. No — trabecular is 80%; uveoscleral is 20%.
Trap: presbyopia is ciliary muscle failure. No — it is hardening of the lens nucleus.

A2Retina & Optic Nerve

Retinal detachment separates photoreceptors from the RPE (the RPE-Bruch's bond is firm). Foveola = pure cones, no rods, capillary-free. Parafovea is thickest; foveola thinnest.
Blood supply: outer retina = choriocapillaris (65–85%); inner retina = central retinal artery. The retina has no pain fibers (painless vision loss).
Numbers: 11 retinal layers; normal C/D ≤0.3; ora serrata 6 mm from limbus; disc ~3 mm nasal to macula; macular pigment = lutein + zeaxanthin.
The optic disc has no photoreceptors (the physiologic blind spot). Glaucoma damages retinal ganglion cell axons at the lamina cribrosa.
Trap: the fovea is the thickest retina. No — the parafovea is thickest; the foveola is thinnest.
Trap: normal C/D is 0.5. No — normal is ≤0.3; 0.5 is suspicious.

A2Visual Pathway

Lesion map: Optic nerve = ipsilateral blindness · Chiasm = bitemporal (pituitary) · Tract = incongruous HH + Wernicke's pupil · LGB onward = congruous.
Radiations: Temporal lobe (Meyer's loop) = superior quadrantanopia ("pie in the sky"); Parietal = inferior quadrantanopia; Occipital = homonymous hemianopia with macular sparing.
Rules: Nasal fibers cross at the chiasm (temporal stay). Congruity increases as lesions move posteriorly. Pupil fibers exit at the pretectum, so cortical lesions spare the light reflex.

A3Ocular Embryology

Origins: Optic vesicle = diencephalon · Lens = surface ectoderm (the only major structure entirely from it) · Retina = neuroectoderm · Corneal stroma + endothelium = neural crest · EOMs = mesoderm.
Fetal fissure closes ~week 7; failure → coloboma, always inferior (6 o'clock). Hyaloid artery regresses by birth (Cloquet's canal remnant).
Vitreous: primary (vascular) regresses → PHPV/leukocoria if it persists; secondary = adult gel; tertiary = lens zonules.
Lens dislocation: Marfan (FBN1) = superior; homocystinuria = inferior. Aniridia = PAX6 mutation.
Trap: the lens comes from neuroectoderm. No — entirely surface ectoderm.
Trap: the secondary vitreous regresses. No — the primary regresses; the secondary becomes the adult gel.
