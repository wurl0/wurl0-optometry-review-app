## Pen and Paper Drills

*These are the exercises to do offline with a pen — not on screen. Active recall only works if you close the note first.*

> **The rule:** Read the section. Close the note. Then do the drill. Only check after you've written something.

---

### Drill 1 — Brain Dump
Write the topic name at the top of a blank page. Set a 5-minute timer. Write everything you remember about this topic without looking — mechanisms, names, numbers, sequences, anything. Then open the note and check what you missed. Circle every gap. Those gaps are your next re-read targets.

**What to brain dump for this note:** [List 2–3 specific things — e.g., "the 5 layers of the cornea in order" or "the 3 steps of the visual cycle"]

---

### Drill 2 — Blank Diagram or Table
Reproduce one of the visual maps or comparison tables from this note — from memory, on paper. Don't sketch loosely. Label everything precisely as if you're the one writing the exam question.

**What to reproduce:** [Specify — e.g., "the structure tree of the ciliary body" or "the DR vs HR comparison table with all rows filled in"]

### Drill 3 — Teach-Back Write
Flip your paper over. Write a 3–5 sentence explanation of this topic as if you're teaching it to a classmate who has never seen it. No jargon you can't define. If you get stuck, that exact spot is where your understanding has a hole.

**Teach-back prompt:** [One sentence framing — e.g., "Explain why accommodation decreases with age without using the word 'presbyopia'"]
