## What This File Is

The PRC publishes two documents that together define the exam:

| Document | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **1994 PRC Syllabi** (what you found) | The granular topic list — every specific concept the board can ask about |
| **2016 TOS** (Table of Specifications) | How many items come from each area and how they are grouped |

The 1994 syllabi is still in force. The 2016 TOS restructured the old subject names into 8 weighted areas (A-H) but the actual content per question still comes from the 1994 syllabi topics. You need both.

The map below answers: **which 1994 syllabi topics go into which folder in this directory.**

## Summary: What the 1994 Syllabi Adds That the TOS Alone Does Not Tell You

| What the TOS says | What the 1994 Syllabi adds |
|---|---|
| "A.2 — The Human Eye" | Every specific structure: corneal layers, iris zones, ciliary body parts, retinal layers — all named and with attributes |
| "B.1 — Visual System and Ocular Optics" | Scheiner's experiment, Purkinje-Sanson images, entoptic phenomena, Donder's table, specific theories |
| "C.3 — Preliminary Examination" | Exact list: VA, ocular motility, pupillary reflexes, dominance, IPD, NPC/NPA |
| "D.1 — Geometric and Physical Optics" | Light theories by name (Fresnel, quantum, corpuscular), specific photometry units, specific prism types |
| "H.1 — Optometry Law" | R.A. 1998 as the base (now updated to R.A. 8050); lists what constitutes malpractice, damages, court appearances |
