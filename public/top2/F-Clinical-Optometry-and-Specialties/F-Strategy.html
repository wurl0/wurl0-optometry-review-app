⚡ Fast Picks — exam shortcuts
Read the options first, kill wrong values, then settle the flip. cue → rule → trap.
Dilation drug — cue: with vs without cycloplegia.
  • ›Dilate without cycloplegia = phenylephrine (α1). Dilate with cycloplegia = tropicamide / cyclopentolate (M3 block).
  • ✗Trap: "phenylephrine causes cycloplegia" — it has none.
Pregnancy dilation — cue: pregnant patient needs dilation.
  • ›Tropicamide (shortest, least exposure) + nasolacrimal occlusion.
  • ✗Trap: phenylephrine 10% (cardiovascular) in pregnancy.
CN3 pupil rule — cue: third-nerve palsy + pupil.
  • ›Pupil-involving = posterior communicating artery aneurysm (neurosurgical emergency). Pupil-spared = ischemic.
  • ✗Trap: observing a pupil-involving CN3 palsy.
Vertical deviation 3-step — cue: a hyperdeviation worse on tilt.
  • ›Parks-Bielschowsky 3-step localizes a CN4 (superior oblique) palsy; head tilts away, chin down.
  • ✗Trap: tilting the head toward the affected side.
Absorptive/UV — cue: UV band or filter.
  • ›UVC highest energy (blocked by ozone); UV400 standard; CPF filters cut blue for specific diagnoses.
  • ✗Trap: calling UVA the highest-energy band.

Subject F — Strategy, Rules & Mnemonics

Clinical Optometry and Specialties · the read-before-you-test reference for all 8 areas

F1Diagnostic Pharmaceutical Agents

5 DPAs: Phenylephrine, Tropicamide, Cyclopentolate, Proparacaine, Fluorescein.
Phenylephrine (α1) dilates with NO cycloplegia. Tropicamide / cyclopentolate (M3 block) dilate AND cycloplege. Tropicamide is preferred in pregnancy; cyclopentolate gives the stronger pediatric cycloplegia (watch CNS effects at 2%).
Proparacaine blocks Na⁺ channels, office-use only (masks corneal damage). Fluorescein glows under cobalt blue (465 nm), never with soft lenses. NLO 2 min limits systemic absorption. All mydriatics: avoid in angle-closure.
Trap: tropicamide dilates without cycloplegia. No — that is phenylephrine; tropicamide does both.

F2Pediatric Optometry

Milestones: eyes straight by 3–4 months. VA tests: infants → preferential looking (Teller); preschool → HOTV / Lea / Allen. Critical period peaks 1–3, closes ~7–8 (some gain to ~12).
Amblyopia order: cycloplegic refraction + full glasses (12–16 wks) FIRST; add patching only if VA doesn't normalize. Deprivation = worst prognosis. Accommodative ET → full cycloplegic plus.
Pseudostrabismus (epicanthal folds) = symmetric Hirschberg reflexes = reassure, no treatment.
Trap: patching is the first amblyopia step. No — full spectacle correction comes first.

F3Geriatric Optometry

AMD: 85% dry / 15% wet, but wet causes most severe vision loss → anti-VEGF. AREDS2 lowers progression risk ~25% (does not reverse). Soft confluent drusen = high risk.
Hypertensive retinopathy (KWB): AV nicking = grade 2; papilledema = grade 4 (emergency). Normal A:V ratio 2:3.
Diabetic retinopathy: most common vision-loss cause = diabetic macular edema (CSME within 500 µm of fovea); severe NPDR = 4-2-1.
Trap: dry AMD causes most severe vision loss. No — wet AMD does, despite being only 15%.

F4Neuro-Optometry

Cranial nerves: pupil-involving CN3 = PCA aneurysm (emergency). CN6 = false localizing sign (raised ICP). CN4 palsy = vertical diplopia worse on downgaze; head tilt away; Parks-Bielschowsky 3-step.
Nystagmus: INS is horizontal in all gaze and dampens with convergence. Vertical nystagmus = always acquired. Latent nystagmus VA test = +5.00 fog (not a patch).
CVI: most common cause = perinatal hypoxia-ischemia; the eyes are structurally normal (normal pupils and OCT); diagnosis is behavioral + neuroimaging.
Trap: occlude one eye to test latent nystagmus. No — occlusion triggers it; fog with +5.00 instead.

F5Occupational Optometry

Chemical splash: irrigate immediately 20–30 min (before identifying the chemical). Safety lens = polycarbonate. Welding UV → photokeratitis; IR → thermal/IR cataract.
CVS: 20-20-20 rule (every 20 min, 20 ft, 20 sec) + AR coating; never prescribe daily home cycloplegics.
Philippine labor: maternity leave (RA 11210) = 105 days; paternity (RA 8187) = 7 days; OSHS under DOLE; GSIS = government, SSS = private. Face shield is used WITH goggles, not instead.
Trap: identify the chemical before irrigating. No — irrigate first; the cause matters less than stopping damage.

F6Sports Vision

Tints: Yellow = fog/haze/low light · Brown/amber = depth perception (golf/tennis) · Gray = neutral density (no contrast gain).
Skills & tools: dynamic VA = tachistoscope / Marsden ball; eye-hand coordination = Wayne Saccadic Fixator; contrast = Pelli-Robson. 20/20 static VA does not measure these.
Protection: polycarbonate eyewear; racquet sports use ASTM F803. Contact lenses give NO impact or UV protection.
Trap: gray tint improves contrast. No — gray is neutral density; brown/amber improves contrast.

F7Low Vision & Rehabilitation

WHO cutoffs: low vision = worse than 20/70; blindness = 20/200 or worse (better eye). Goal = functional vision for the task, NOT 20/20.
Magnification math: reference 4 D = 1×; M = required VA / present VA; equivalent add = M × 4. (N32 → N8 = 4× = +16 D, WD ~6 cm.)
Devices: telescopes = distance (most patients need near first); stand magnifier for tremor/sustained reading. Eccentric viewing helps any central scotoma. Calculate from best-corrected VA.
Trap: all low vision patients need telescopes. No — most need near magnification first.

F8Prosthesis & Absorptive Lenses

Prosthetics: conformer = temporary post-op placeholder; custom PMMA prosthesis (certified ocularist) fitted at 6–8 weeks; polish every 6–12 months, replace every 5–7 years. Scleral shell = over a shrunken globe (not after enucleation). Soak in sterile saline, not tap water.
Absorptive filters: CPF 527 (orange) = RP / macular disease / glaucoma · achromatopsia = deep orange/red · albinism = deep absorptive + UV 400.
Photochromic lenses do not darken in a car (the windshield blocks UV).
Trap: a scleral shell is used after enucleation. No — it fits over a shrunken globe; after enucleation a custom prosthesis sits over the implant.
