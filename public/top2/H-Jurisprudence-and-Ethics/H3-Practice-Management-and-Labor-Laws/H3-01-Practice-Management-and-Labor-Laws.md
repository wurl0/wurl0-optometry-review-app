## H3 — Practice Management and Labor Laws
*Personal study notes | TOS: H Jurisprudence and Ethics | Source: Ethics x Practice Management (Board Resolutions) + RA 8050*

---

## The Simple Version

Practice management is the business and regulatory side of running a clinic. Three blocks:

1. **Setting up the business:** the government registrations every clinic needs (DTI, Barangay, Mayor, BIR).
2. **Running a compliant clinic:** what the **Board Resolution No. 11 (Series of 2016)** requires of your office (instruments, room size, displays, no commercialization).
3. **Advertising and conduct:** what you may and may not say to attract patients (**Board Resolution No. 1, Series of 2008**, and RA 8050 Sec 32).

**Teaching angle:** the recurring theme is **professionalism over commercialization**. Almost every rule exists to keep optometry a profession, not a retail counter. When unsure on an exam item, pick the answer that protects professional dignity.

---

## But Why?

**Why so many separate registrations to open one clinic?**
Each agency licenses a different thing: **DTI** registers your business **name**, the **Barangay** clears your **location**, the **Mayor** issues the **business permit** to operate, and the **BIR** registers you for **taxes**. They stack because each level of government has its own authority over a part of running a business.

**Why must the exam room be enclosed, a set size, and free of the product display?**
Because the law wants the clinical act separated from the sales act. A defined, professional examination space (minimum **10 square meters**, **3-meter** test distance) signals that the patient is receiving health care, not shopping. Frames and sunglasses must not be used to identify or highlight the practice, to protect optometry's image against commercialization.

**Why is chair time the sole discretion of the doctor?**
Because clinical decisions must be free of commercial pressure. The optical's manager or owner cannot tell the optometrist to rush an exam to see more customers. The doctor decides the method, procedure, and time; that independence is what makes it a profession.

**Why are price and "free service" ads prohibited?**
Because competing on price and giveaways turns eye care into a discount race, which degrades standards and dignity. The law allows only **true, honest, dignified, objective** information. You may say what you do, not that you are cheaper or better than colleagues.

### 1. Setting Up the Business (sole proprietorship)

| Step | Agency | What you get |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **DTI** (Department of Trade and Industry) | Business **name** registration (submit up to 3 name choices; certificate valid **5 years**) |
| 2 | **Barangay** | Barangay clearance / certificate of business registration (pay community tax) |
| 3 | **Mayor's office** | **Mayor's / business permit** (after inspections: fire, sanitation, electrical, etc.) |
| 4 | **BIR** (Bureau of Internal Revenue) | Certificate of registration, **TIN**, and registered **books of accounts** |

### 2. Compliant Clinic (Board Resolution No. 11, Series of 2016)

**Office/Clinic (Rule VII, Sec 44):**
- Notify the Board of your clinic location and submit photographs within **30 days** of securing the premises; report any change of location.
- The clinic must be used **exclusively** for optometry, enclosed and apart from commercial concerns.
- **Display:** names and specializations of the optometrists, valid CORs, updated PICs (professional ID cards), Certificates of Good Standing, and CPD certificates. Only optometry-related certificates and diplomas may be shown.
- The clinic must be **manned by a licensed optometrist during operating hours**.

**Instruments and Equipment (Sec 46):**
- **Ocular exam:** slit-lamp, ophthalmoscope, retinoscope, phoropter, trial lens set, VA chart, Ishihara plates, Amsler grid, contrast sensitivity chart, tangent screen.
- **Binocular vision:** penlight + occluder, prism bar + loose prisms, Maddox wing, RAF rule, Worth Four Dot, Stereo Fly.
- **Contact lens:** keratometer (or topographer), CL trial lenses.
- **Optional:** Schiotz/Goldmann tonometer, automated tangent screen, automated perimeter.

**Physical Facilities (Sec 47):**
- **Examination/treatment room:** minimum **10 square meters**, test distance at least **3 meters**, optometrist's name posted outside.
- **Receiving area:** for registration and forms; only optometry-related certificates displayed.
- **Product display area:** frames/sunglasses **must not be used to highlight or identify the practice**; no commercialization; no advertisement of free services.
- **Ophthalmic laboratory:** separate from the exam room.
- Locker rooms, pantry, stockroom must be separate from the service area.

**Service delivery (Sec 5, Board Res 11):** minimum services for school-aged children, adults (18+), and seniors (60+); management options include spectacle correction, safety eyewear, contact lenses, and basic vision therapy.

**Time and Manner of Examination (Sec 48):** chair time, method, and procedure are the **sole discretion of the handling doctor**, free from external influence (owners, managers, staff). Wear professional attire.

### 3. Advertising and Conduct

**RA 8050 Sec 32(c)** and **Board Resolution No. 1 (Series of 2008), Rule VII Sec 39:**
- An optometrist may use **only true, honest, fair, dignified, objective** information.
- **Prohibited:** self-laudatory claims (better/superior services, exclusivity); any reference to **fees** or lower-than-customary prices; advertising **free exams, discounts, bonuses, premiums, prizes**; exaggerated or false warranties; undignified, immodest, or vulgar ads.
- **Prohibited solicitation:** canvassing; employing solicitors, agents, entertainers, or lecturers to attract patronage; using groups or organizations to solicit patients.

### 4. Labor and Statutory Obligations (practical)

An optometrist who employs staff (optical assistants, technicians) is an employer with basic obligations under Philippine labor standards:
- Mandatory government contributions: **SSS, PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG** for employees.
- Basic standards: minimum wage, statutory benefits (13th month pay, holiday/overtime premiums), and safe working conditions.
- The optometrist remains professionally responsible for delegated tasks (**respondeat superior**, see [[G1-01-General-Pharmacology-Principles]]): a non-optometrist staff member may not perform the acts that constitute the practice of optometry ([[H1-01-RA8050-Optometry-Law]]).

## Board Traps

- **Trap:** you may advertise a free eye exam to attract new patients. Wrong. Advertising free or discounted services is a prohibited act.
- **Trap:** the optical owner can set how long each exam takes to improve turnover. Wrong. Chair time is the **sole discretion of the optometrist**, free of external influence.
- **Trap:** you can register with just the Mayor's permit. Wrong. You need DTI (name), Barangay, Mayor, and BIR.
- **Trap:** the exam room minimum is 5 square meters. Wrong. **10 square meters**, with at least a **3-meter** test distance.
- **Trap:** displaying many frames out front helps identify your clinic professionally. Wrong. Product displays must **not** be used to highlight or identify the practice (anti-commercialization).
- **Trap:** you have a year to inform the Board of your clinic location. Wrong. **30 days**.

## Practice Questions

**1.** What is the correct order of registrations to open a sole-proprietorship optometry clinic?

- A. BIR → Mayor → Barangay → DTI
- B. DTI → Barangay → Mayor → BIR
- C. Mayor → DTI → BIR → Barangay
- D. Barangay → BIR → DTI → Mayor

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B. DTI → Barangay → Mayor → BIR.** Register the name (DTI), clear the location (Barangay), get the permit (Mayor), then register for taxes (BIR).
</details>

**2.** An optical owner instructs the optometrist to limit each eye exam to ten minutes to serve more customers. This violates:

- A. Nothing; the owner runs the business
- B. The rule that chair time is the sole discretion of the handling doctor
- C. Advertising rules
- D. Labor law

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Board Resolution No. 11 (Sec 48) makes chair time, method, and procedure the sole discretion of the optometrist, free from external influence by owners or managers.
</details>

**3.** Which advertisement is permitted under the rules?

- A. "Lowest eyeglass prices in the city"
- B. "Free eye exam with every purchase"
- C. A dignified statement of the services the clinic offers
- D. "The best optometrist in the region"

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Only true, honest, dignified, objective information is allowed. Price claims, free-service offers, and self-laudatory superiority claims are prohibited.
</details>

**4.** The minimum examination room area and test distance required are:

- A. 5 sq m and 6 m
- B. 10 sq m and 3 m
- C. 20 sq m and 6 m
- D. 8 sq m and 4 m

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B. 10 square meters with at least a 3-meter test distance** (Board Resolution No. 11, Sec 47).
</details>

**5.** After securing clinic premises, within how many days must the optometrist notify the Board of the location?

- A. 15 days
- B. 30 days
- C. 60 days
- D. 90 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B. 30 days**, with photographs, to allow inspection and monitoring.
</details>

## Fast Picks — Exam Shortcuts

Read the options first, kill wrong values, then settle the flip. `cue → rule → trap`.

**The numbers** — cue: any RA 8050 figure.
- Pass = **75% avg, none < 50%**. Board = **5 from 10**, 5-yr non-renewable, quorum 3. License renews **every 3 yrs**. Appeal **15 days**. Penalty **1–8 yrs / ₱10k–40k**.
- Trap: "license renews every 5 years" (that's the **board term**, not the license — license is 3).

**Which law is current** — cue: RA 8050 vs RA 1998.
- RA 8050 (1995) is current; it **repealed** RA 1998.
- Trap: "RA 1998 is newer" because the number is bigger.

**Four ethics principles** — cue: the scenario's act.
- Consent/refusal = autonomy; refer beyond scope = non-maleficence; equal care regardless of status = justice; act in best interest = beneficence.
- Trap: matching "refer when indicated" to beneficence — it's **non-maleficence**.

**Advertising** — cue: an ad claim.
- Allowed = true/honest/dignified statement of services. Prohibited = price, "better/best," free exams/discounts.
- Trap: "lower-fee ad is fine if it's true" — truth is no defense; it's prohibited.

**Clinic + setup** — cue: a facility number or registration.
- Exam room **≥10 sq m, ≥3 m** test distance; notify Board within **30 days**. Setup order: **DTI → Barangay → Mayor → BIR**.
- Trap: a 5 sq m room or starting with the Mayor's permit.
