Which law is current — cue: RA 8050 vs RA 1998.
  • ›RA 8050 (1995) is current; it repealed RA 1998.
  • ✗Trap: "RA 1998 is newer" because the number is bigger.
Four ethics principles — cue: the scenario's act.
  • ›Consent/refusal = autonomy; refer beyond scope = non-maleficence; equal care regardless of status = justice; act in best interest = beneficence.
  • ✗Trap: matching "refer when indicated" to beneficence — it's non-maleficence.
Advertising — cue: an ad claim.
  • ›Allowed = true/honest/dignified statement of services. Prohibited = price, "better/best," free exams/discounts.
  • ✗Trap: "lower-fee ad is fine if it's true" — truth is no defense; it's prohibited.
Clinic + setup — cue: a facility number or registration.
  • ›Exam room ≥10 sq m, ≥3 m test distance; notify Board within 30 days. Setup order: DTI → Barangay → Mayor → BIR.
  • ✗Trap: a 5 sq m room or starting with the Mayor's permit.

Scope exceptions (Sec 5): physicians with post-graduate eye training are exempt; public health workers do visual acuity + screening only. Regulator = PRC. DPA use needs pharmacology certification (Sec 31). Code of Ethics = Sec 40 (6 duties).
Trap: RA 1998 is newer than RA 8050. No — the numbers mislead; RA 8050 (1995) is current and repealed RA 1998.

H2Bioethics & Code of Ethics

Four principles "A-B-N-J" (Beauchamp & Childress): Autonomy (informed consent), Beneficence (do good), Non-maleficence (do no harm), Justice (equal care).
Sec 40 dutyPrinciple
Visual welfare of the publicBeneficence
Equal diligence to allJustice
ConfidentialityAutonomy / trust
Refer when indicatedNon-maleficence
Consent: requires understanding of diagnosis, options, risks, alternatives (not a mere signature). Minor → guardian consents (child assents); emergency → may be waived. Refusal of referral → document, re-counsel, do not abandon.
Doctrines: respondeat superior ('let the master answer'); informed consent; guardian consent for minors/incompetents. Most-tested principles: autonomy (consent) and non-maleficence (referral/contraindications).
Trap: non-maleficence means avoiding all risk. No — the expected benefit must outweigh the expected harm.

H3Practice Management & Labor Laws

Business setup "D-B-M-B": DTI (name, 5-yr cert) → Barangay (clearance) → Mayor (business permit) → BIR (TIN, books of accounts).
Board Resolution No. 11 (2016): notify the Board of clinic location within 30 days; exam room ≥ 10 sq m with ≥ 3 m test distance; display COR/PIC/COGS/CPD; clinic used exclusively for optometry and manned by a licensed optometrist; chair time = the optometrist's sole discretion.
Advertising: only true, honest, dignified, objective information. Prohibited: price/comparative claims, free-service offers, and soliciting (canvassing). Product displays must not identify the practice (anti-commercialization).
Labor: SSS (private) / GSIS (government) + PhilHealth + Pag-IBIG. Maternity = 105 days (RA 11210); paternity = 7 days (RA 8187); OSHS under DOLE.
Trap: the optical owner sets exam time to boost turnover. No — chair time is the optometrist's sole discretion, free of external influence.
Trap: you may advertise a free eye exam. No — free-service advertising is prohibited.
