## H1 — RA 8050: The Revised Optometry Law of 1995
*Personal study notes | TOS: H Jurisprudence and Ethics | Source: Republic Act 8050 full text*

---

## The Simple Version

**RA 8050** is the **Revised Optometry Law of 1995**. It is the single law that defines what optometry is, who may practice it, how you get licensed, who regulates you, and what gets you punished. It **repealed the old RA 1998**.

Think of it as answering five questions:
1. **What** is the practice of optometry? (the acts you are allowed to do)
2. **Who** may practice? (registered, licensed optometrists only, with narrow exceptions)
3. **How** do you become one? (the licensure exam and registration)
4. **Who watches over you?** (the Board of Optometry, under the PRC)
5. **What is forbidden, and what is the penalty?** (prohibited acts and penal provisions)

**Teaching angle:** the board loves the numbers (5 board members, 75% passing, 3-year renewal, 1 to 8 years jail). Lock the numbers first; they are the cheapest marks in jurisprudence.

---

## But Why?

**Why does optometry need its own law at all?**
Because optometrists use diagnostic drugs and make clinical decisions that affect health. The State declares optometry part of **primary health care**, so it regulates entry and conduct to protect the public, the same reason medicine and pharmacy are licensed.

**Why can a physician examine the eye without an optometry license, but a public health worker can only do a visual acuity test?**
The law protects scope by training. A physician with post-graduate eye training is more qualified, so examining the eye is allowed and is not "practicing optometry." A public health worker in a blindness-prevention program is trained only for screening, so they may do **visual acuity and screening only**, nothing more. Scope follows competence.

**Why does the law require a separate pharmacology certification before using DPAs?**
Diagnostic drugs are real pharmaceuticals with real risks (see [[F1-01-DPA-Examination]] and [[G2-01-Autonomic-Drugs]]). Section 31 requires those trained before the law to pass a special pharmacology certification before they may instill DPAs. Using DPAs untrained is a prohibited act, injury or not.

**Why is the Board term non-renewable and staggered?**
Five-year non-renewable terms, with one member rotating out each year, keep the Board independent and continuous: no one entrenches, and experience is never lost all at once. The senior member becomes chairperson in the final year.

### What the Law Is

- **Title:** Revised Optometry Law of 1995 (RA 8050). **Repealed RA 1998** (the old Optometry Law).
- **State policy:** optometry is an essential profession and a regular component of the **primary health care system**.
- **Optometry (definition):** the science and art of examining the eye, analyzing ocular function, prescribing and dispensing lenses/prisms/contact lenses/low-vision aids, conducting ocular exercises and orthoptics, installing prosthetics, using authorized DPAs, and other measures for the aid, correction, or relief of the human eye.

### Historical Milestones (jurisprudence background)

The board loves "firsts" and the law lineage. Source: Cinco, "Optometry: Legal and Professional Aspects."

- **Law lineage:** Optometry Law of **1917** (first, written in Spanish; amended 1919) to **RA 1998** (1957) to **RA 8050** (1995). The 1917 law created the **Junta Examinadora** (Board of Examiners), later renamed the **Board of Optometry** by PD 223 (as amended by PD 657).
- **First optometric clinic with refraction + first lens-grinding shop:** R.M. Clark, Manila, **1902**.
- **First optometry school:** Philippine College of Optometry (to 1925). **First university-affiliated college:** Centro Escolar University, **1928** (first dean Dr. Roy A. Thorson).
- **Registration Certificate No. 1** (per PRC records): issued **March 11, 1918 to Dr. Manuel Sabater**.
- **Associations:** OAP (1936, Justo Gonzalez first president) + PODA + PROA merged in **1979** into **Samahan Ng Optometrist Sa Pilipinas (SOP)**, the integrated national body.
- **Regional/world:** **IFAPAO** founded at the 1st APOC (Manila, **1978**); later became **APCO**. IOOL became the **WCO**. First **International Optometrist of the Year** (1988) was **Dr. Claro M. Cinco**.
- Deeper coverage in the **Law Book (full)** reviewer linked from the H reviewer bar.

### Acts Constituting Practice (Sec 4)

(a) Examining the eye (subjective + objective, with DPAs and instruments); (b) prescribing and dispensing lenses, prisms, contact lenses, frames; (c) ocular exercises, vision training, orthoptics, prosthetics; (d) counseling on vision and eye care; (e) establishing offices/clinics; (f) collecting professional fees for acts a to d.

### Who May Practice (Sec 5 exceptions)

Only registered, licensed optometrists. **Exceptions:**
- Physicians with **post-graduate eye training** (and a physician's eye exam during a physical is not "practicing optometry").
- **Public health workers** in the blindness-prevention program: **visual acuity test and visual screening only**.

**Disclosure (Sec 6):** indicate license number and expiry on documents; **display the certificate of registration** conspicuously in the clinic.

### The Board of Optometry

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Members | **5**, appointed by the **President** from **10 nominees** of the accredited national organization |
| Term | **5 years, non-renewable**, staggered (one rotates out yearly; senior becomes chair in final year) |
| Quorum | **3 members** |
| Votes for an administrative decision | **3 members** |
| Supervision | Under the **PRC** (Professional Regulation Commission) |
| Removal | By the President for neglect, incompetence, malpractice, or unethical conduct |

### Licensure (Sec 17 to 29)

- Exam given **at least once a year** by the Board.
- **Requirements (Sec 19):** Filipino citizen, good moral character, **Doctor of Optometry (O.D.)** from an accredited school after a **6-year course**.
- **Passing (Sec 20): general average of at least 75%, with no subject below 50%.**
- Results reported to PRC within **60 days**.
- **Fail 3 times → must take a refresher course** before re-examination (Sec 22).
- Take the **oath**, then PRC issues the **certificate of registration** and **professional license**.
- **License renewal every 3 years** with continuing education and fees (Sec 29).
- **Appeals (Sec 27):** appeal a revocation/suspension to the PRC within **15 days**.

### DPAs and Pharmacology (Sec 31)

Optometrists practicing before the law must take a pharmacology course and pass a **special certification exam** before using DPAs. The **5 approved DPAs** are detailed in [[F1-01-DPA-Examination]]: phenylephrine, tropicamide, cyclopentolate, proparacaine, fluorescein (plus miotics).

### Prohibited Acts (Sec 32) and Penalties (Sec 33)

**Prohibited:** (a) false evidence to the Board; (b) posing as a registered optometrist without a license; (c) **comparative/price advertising** ("better than others," cheaper fees/lenses); (d) DPA use by an untrained optometrist; (e) using unauthorized drugs; (f) unauthorized practice; (g) any violation of the Act or its rules.

**Penalty (Sec 33):** imprisonment of **1 to 8 years**, or a fine of **P10,000 to P40,000**, or **both**, at the court's discretion. Administrative penalties (Sec 26 revocation/suspension) also apply.

### Integration and Ethics (Sec 36 to 40)

- All optometrists are integrated into **one national organization**, which promulgates the **Code of Professional Ethics** (adopted by the Board, approved by PRC).
- Six ethical duties (Sec 40): primary responsibility to the **visual welfare of the public**; promote best standards; continuously improve proficiency; serve all equally regardless of race/creed/status; respect **confidentiality**; and **refer** when medical or other attention is needed. (Expanded in [[H2-01-Bioethics-and-Code-of-Ethics]].)

## Board Traps

- **Trap:** RA 8050 repealed RA 1998, so 1998 is newer. Wrong. The numbers mislead. **RA 8050 (1995) is the current law**; RA 1998 was the older one it replaced.
- **Trap:** a public health worker may dispense glasses under the blindness program. Wrong. Only **visual acuity and screening**.
- **Trap:** the Board has 7 members. Wrong. **5**, from 10 nominees.
- **Trap:** passing is a 75% average with no rule on individual subjects. Wrong. No subject may fall **below 50%**.
- **Trap:** license renews every 5 years (like the Board term). Wrong. **3 years**.
- **Trap:** advertising lower fees is allowed if true. Wrong. Comparative and price advertising is a **prohibited act**.
- **Trap:** appeals have 30 days. Wrong. **15 days** to the PRC.
- **Trap:** Registration Certificate No. 1 went to the reputed "first," Federico Sarabia. Wrong per PRC records. It was issued to **Dr. Manuel Sabater** (March 11, 1918).
- **Trap:** RA 8050 lists 6 DPAs. As originally written it lists **5**; the **6th (Cyclopentolate)** came from the **2008 IRR**. Match the count to what the question asks.
- **Trap:** RA 8050 still requires CPD to renew a license. That Sec. 29 requirement was **repealed by Sec. 20 of RA 8981** (PRC Modernization Act of 2000).

## Practice Questions

**1.** A candidate scores a 78% general average but gets 48% in one subject. Under RA 8050, the candidate:

- A. Passes (average is above 75%)
- B. Fails (a subject is below 50%)
- C. Passes conditionally
- D. Must retake only that subject

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B. Fails.** Section 20 requires at least a 75% average AND no rating below 50% in any subject. The 48% disqualifies despite the high average.
</details>

**2.** Who may legally examine the human eye without an optometry license under RA 8050?

- A. Any pharmacist
- B. A physician with post-graduate eye training
- C. An optician
- D. A contact lens salesperson

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Section 5 exempts physicians with post-graduate training in diagnosis and treatment of eye disease. Public health workers may do only visual acuity testing and screening.
</details>

**3.** The Board of Optometry is composed of how many members, and from how many nominees?

- A. 3 members from 6 nominees
- B. 5 members from 10 nominees
- C. 7 members from 14 nominees
- D. 5 members from 5 nominees

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B. 5 members from 10 nominees**, appointed by the President on PRC recommendation, from the accredited national organization's list.
</details>

**4.** An optometrist places an ad claiming "lower fees than any clinic in town." Under RA 8050 this is:

- A. Allowed if the claim is true
- B. A prohibited act
- C. Allowed with Board approval
- D. Allowed only in print media

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B. A prohibited act.** Section 32(c) prohibits advertising conveying that one optometrist is better or charges lower fees. Truth is no defense.
</details>

**5.** A person convicted of unauthorized practice of optometry faces what penalty?

- A. Fine only, up to P5,000
- B. Imprisonment of 1 to 8 years, or fine of P10,000 to P40,000, or both
- C. License suspension only
- D. Community service

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Section 33 sets imprisonment of 1 to 8 years, or a fine of P10,000 to P40,000, or both, at the court's discretion, plus applicable administrative penalties.
</details>
