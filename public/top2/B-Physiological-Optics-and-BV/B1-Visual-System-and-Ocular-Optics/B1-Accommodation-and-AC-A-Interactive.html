Helmholtz: how the lens rounds up

For near, the ciliary muscle contracts. That releases tension on the zonules, and the elastic lens springs into a rounder, more powerful shape. It feels backwards: muscle contraction loosens the zonules. With age the lens stiffens and stops responding, which is presbyopia.

AC/A ratio

Accommodation drags convergence along with it. The AC/A ratio is how many prism dioptres of accommodative convergence you get per dioptre of accommodation. Normal is about 3 to 5 Δ/D. Too high or too low drives a near eso or exo deviation.
