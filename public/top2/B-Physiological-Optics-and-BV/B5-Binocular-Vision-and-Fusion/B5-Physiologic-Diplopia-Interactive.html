1. Near crosses, far doesn't

Top-down view. Both eyes stay fixated on the green point. Slide the orange object nearer or farther and watch what each eye perceives (red = right eye, blue = left eye).

2. Retinal points & the cyclopean eye

Where the object's image lands on each retina, the matching (corresponding) pairs, and how the brain projects both images from the single "cyclopean" eye. Toggle the object beyond vs nearer than fixation.

Corresponding pairs: fovea ↔ fovea (straight ahead), and the nasal retina of one eye ↔ temporal retina of the other. A non-fixated object lands on same-named points (both nasal, or both temporal) = disparate → seen double.
