Top-down view. The horopter is the curve of points that land on corresponding retinal points, so they are seen single. Panum's area is the thin zone around it where slightly disparate images still fuse. Drag the pink dot. Inside the band it stays single; outside it splits into double vision (physiologic diplopia), crossed when nearer than fixation, uncrossed when farther.

Why it matters

Panum's area is small centrally (a few arc-minutes) and wider in the periphery, which is why fine stereopsis is best near fixation. Objects outside Panum's area give the disparity your brain reads as depth up to a limit, then they double. Hold a finger up close while fixating something far: the finger doubles (crossed diplopia), a normal demonstration of this exact geometry.
