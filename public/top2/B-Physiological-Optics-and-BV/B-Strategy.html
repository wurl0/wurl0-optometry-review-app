Color theory by level — cue: "receptor" vs "post-receptor" vs "combined".
  • ›Receptor = trichromatic (Young-Helmholtz). Post-receptor = opponent (Hering). Combined = stage (Ladd-Franklin).
  • ✗Trap: assigning opponent-process to the receptor level.
Sheard vs Percival — cue: criterion name.
  • ›Sheard = fusional reserve ≥ 2× the phoria (for symptomatic). Percival = phoria in the middle third of the vergence range (independent of phoria).
  • ✗Trap: swapping the two definitions.
Worth-4-dot fusion grade — cue: simultaneous / fusion / stereo.
  • ›Grade 1 = simultaneous macular perception, Grade 2 = flat fusion, Grade 3 = stereopsis.
  • ✗Trap: calling stereopsis "Grade 2."
Physiologic diplopia — cue: near vs far target.
  • ›Far object behind fixation = uncrossed (homonymous); near object = crossed.
  • ✗Trap: reversing crossed/uncrossed.
AC/A — cue: deviation worse at near vs distance.
  • ›Worse at near = high AC/A (convergence excess); worse at distance = divergence excess.
  • ✗Trap: matching "convergence insufficiency" to a high AC/A — CI is low AC/A.

B1Visual System & Ocular Optics

Axes "ATV 24-22-22": Anteroposterior = Torsion (24 mm, longest); Transverse = Vertical movement (22); Vertical = Horizontal movement (22). All pass through the center of rotation, 13.5 mm behind the cornea.
Cone pigments "ECG": Erythrolabe (red, ~750 nm), Chlorolabe (green, 535 nm), Cyanolabe (blue, 440 nm).
507 vs 555: rods peak 507 nm (scotopic, blue-green); cones peak 555 nm (photopic, yellow-green). The Purkinje shift moves the peak 555 → 507 as light fades.
Dark adaptation: cones plateau at ~7 min (Kohlrausch kink); rods finish at 30–40 min, ending ~1000× (3 log units) more sensitive.
Angle kappa clue: positive kappa → pupil nasal → reflex temporal → pseudo-exo. Negative kappa → reflex nasal → pseudo-eso. Alpha is measured at the nodal point, kappa at the pupil.
Trap: the fovea has the most rods. No — rods are absent at the fovea (highest cone density). Rods peak ~20° out (averted vision).
Trap: full dark adaptation takes 7 min. No — that is cones; full rod adaptation is 30–40 min.

B2Visual Perception

LGB layers "1-4-6 cross, 2-3-5 stay": layers 1, 4, 6 = contralateral (crossed/nasal); layers 2, 3, 5 = ipsilateral (uncrossed/temporal).
Streams: Ventral = "What" (temporal lobe, object ID). Dorsal = "Where/How" (parietal lobe, space/motion).
Color is most vulnerable, motion most resilient. Color fails first in optic nerve disease (basis of red-desaturation test).
Cortex 17-18-19: 17 = primary striate (basic features), 18 = parastriate, 19 = peristriate. Recognition needs ventral stream beyond 17.
Weber's law: ΔI/I = k. The JND is a constant fraction of background, so it doubles when the background doubles. Basis of contrast sensitivity.
Trap: area 17 recognizes objects. No — it detects basic features; recognition is a ventral-stream (18/19/IT) function.

B3Visual Field

Extents "Temporal is widest": Temporal 100°, Inferior 75°, Superior 60°, Nasal 60°.
120 + 30: central 120° is binocular; each side has a 30° monocular temporal crescent.
Goldmann: V = largest (64 mm²), I = smallest (0.25 mm²); bowl 300 mm radius, background 31.5 apostilbs.
Amsler = central 10° (1 square = 1°); a macular test.

Mixing: Additive light R+G+B = White; Subtractive pigment C+M+Y = Black.
Most common defect = deuteranomaly (weak green). Red-green deficiency ~8% of males. Tritan defects are rare and autosomal.
Tests: Ishihara screens red-green (plate 1 = control everyone sees); HRR tests red-green AND blue-yellow; FM-100 Hue = most comprehensive (axis + severity); FALANT = occupational lantern (8 ft).
Trap: additive mixing gives black. No — additive (light) = white; subtractive (pigment) = black.

B5Binocular Vision & Fusion (highest yield)

Normal AC/A = 4:1 to 6:1. High AC/A = Excess (Convergence Excess, Divergence Excess). Low AC/A = Insufficiency (Convergence Insufficiency, Divergence Insufficiency).
Sheard's 2:1: fusional reserve ≥ 2× the phoria. Percival's: phoria in the middle third of the range.
Worth "Super-Flat-Stereo": Grade 1 simultaneous perception, Grade 2 flat fusion, Grade 3 stereopsis.
Laws: Hering = binocular yoke muscles (equal innervation); Sherrington = monocular agonist/antagonist (reciprocal).
Physiologic diplopia "Far Uncrossed, Near Crossed." Eye-movement speed: vergence slowest (20°/s); VOR fastest reflex (15 ms); saccade fastest voluntary (500–1000°/s).
NPC normal ≤8 cm (>10 = CI). Amblyopia "SAD-Am": Strabismic (most common), Anisometropic, Deprivation (worst prognosis), Ametropic, Meridional.
Trap: high AC/A causes convergence insufficiency. No — CI has a low AC/A. High AC/A drives the excess conditions.
Trap: Hering's law is monocular. No — Hering = binocular yoke; Sherrington = monocular.

B6Stereopsis & Depth

Normal stereoacuity = 20–40 sec arc. Smaller arc-seconds = finer. Stereopsis = Worth Grade 3 (needs Grades 1 + 2 first).
Stereopsis = binocular retinal disparity. It develops ~3–6 months (not at birth) and is absent in deep constant strabismic amblyopia.
Tests: Frisby = real depth, no glasses; Titmus fly = coarsest (3000 sec arc); Randot = common clinical (down to ~20 sec arc).
Trap: larger arc-seconds = better stereo. No — smaller is finer (20 beats 400).
Trap: losing one eye removes all depth perception. No — monocular cues (perspective, interposition, motion parallax) remain.

B7VIP / VPS & Cognitive Processing

VIP is broad (processing + motor); VPS is the non-motor subset. 20/20 acuity does NOT rule out a VIP deficit.

Decision rule: poor Beery VMI → run TVPS-3. If TVPS-3 normal, the deficit is motor; if poor, it is perceptual.
Subtopics: figure-ground (find object in clutter), form constancy (same shape, changed size/orientation), visual closure (whole from partial), plus discrimination, spatial relations, visual memory, sequential memory.
Trap: 20/20 acuity means no perceptual problem. No — perception is separate from acuity.
