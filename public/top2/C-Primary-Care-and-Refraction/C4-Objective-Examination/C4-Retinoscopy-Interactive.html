Retinoscopy — With, Against, Neutral

Board Review 2026 · Personal study notes · sweep the streak, read the reflex, find neutral

You sweep the retinoscope streak across the pupil and watch the reflex inside it. With motion (reflex moves the same way) means add plus. Against motion means add minus. When the pupil fills with light and stops moving, you are at neutral: the far point sits at your eye.

How to read it

With movement: the far point is behind your peephole, the eye needs more plus. Against movement: the far point is in front of you, the eye needs more minus. As you approach neutral the reflex gets wider, brighter, and faster; at neutral it fills the whole pupil. Then subtract your working-distance lens to get the refraction: Rx = trial lens − working-distance dioptres.
