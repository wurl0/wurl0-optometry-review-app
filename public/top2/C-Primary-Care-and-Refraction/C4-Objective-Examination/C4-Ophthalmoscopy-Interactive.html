The two numbers that flip

Magnification ≈ power of the eye ÷ power of the lens. The eye is about 60 D. Direct ophthalmoscopy is roughly 60 ÷ 4 ≈ 15×. With indirect, a lower-power lens (14 D) gives more magnification but a narrow field; a higher-power lens (28 D) gives a wide field but less magnification. Field of view always moves opposite to magnification.

Because the indirect image is inverted and reversed (rotated 180°), a lesion that looks superior is really inferior, which is why BIO fundus drawings are charted upside-down.
