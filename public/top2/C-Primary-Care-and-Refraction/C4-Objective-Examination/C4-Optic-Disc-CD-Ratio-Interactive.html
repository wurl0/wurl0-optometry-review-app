What you are grading at the disc

With the direct ophthalmoscope, judge: the vertical C/D ratio, the neuroretinal rim (the ISNT rule: rim thickest Inferior, then Superior, then Nasal, thinnest Temporal), rim colour/pallor, and any disc haemorrhage. A C/D asymmetry > 0.2 between the two eyes is suspicious even if each disc looks borderline.
