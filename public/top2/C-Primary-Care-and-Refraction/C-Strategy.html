⚡ Fast Picks — exam shortcuts
Read the options first, kill wrong values, then settle the flip. cue → rule → trap.
JCC axis/power — cue: which dot position the patient prefers.
  • ›Prefers P1 (red dots ∥ tentative axis) → cyl under-powered → add minus cyl. Prefers P2 (white dots ∥ axis) → reduce minus.
  • ✗Trap: adding cylinder when the patient actually preferred P2.
Duochrome — cue: red side vs green side clearer.
  • ›Green clearer → over-minused → add plus / reduce minus. Red clearer → add minus. (RAM-GAP: Red Add Minus, Green Add Plus.)
  • ✗Trap: over-minusing a patient who says green is clearer.
Cover test phoria vs tropia — cue: movement on cover vs without cover.
  • ›Moves only when covered (recovers on uncover) = phoria. Moves without cover = tropia.
  • ✗Trap: calling a phoria a tropia because the eye moved — a phoria only moves under cover.
Prism for phoria — cue: exo / eso / hyper.
  • ›Exophoria → BI, esophoria → BO, hyperphoria → BD before the higher eye.
  • ✗Trap: base-out for exo (it's base-in).
Retinoscopy reflex — cue: with vs against motion.
  • ›With motion → add plus (under-plussed/hyperopic). Against → add minus. Neutral = endpoint (+ working distance).
  • ✗Trap: forgetting to subtract the working-distance lens at the end.

C1Epidemiology & Prevention

Headline fact: the two leading causes of global vision impairment are uncorrected refractive error and cataract, and over 80% of VI is avoidable.
EPIC sizes: Endemic (constant, low) < Epidemic (regional outbreak) < Pandemic (across regions). Incidence = new cases; prevalence = all existing cases.
Prevention 1-2-3: primary = prevent onset; secondary = detect/reverse early; tertiary = limit disability. Philippines: cataract is the leading cause of blindness. WHO founded April 7, 1948.
Trap: incidence and prevalence are the same. No — incidence is new cases over time; prevalence is all existing cases at a point.

C2Ametropia & Refractive Errors

Corrections: myopia → minus; hyperopia → plus; astigmatism → cylinder; presbyopia → plus add.
Hofstetter: Min = 15 − 0.25(age); Ave = 18.5 − 0.30(age); Max = 25 − 0.40(age). Far point: emmetrope = ∞; −3 D myope = 33 cm; hyperope = virtual (behind eye).
Astigmatism: WTR steep at 90° (youth); ATR steep at 180° (age). Total hyperopia = latent + manifest. Pseudomyopia = accommodative spasm that clears under cycloplegia.
Retinoscopy: at 67 cm subtract 1.50 D; with motion = add plus, against motion = add minus. Hyperope + eso at near + high AC/A = accommodative esotropia (treat with full plus).
Trap: hyperopes always see distance clearly. No — absolute hyperopes are blurred at all distances.
Trap: WTR is more common in the elderly. No — WTR is common in youth; ATR increases with age.

C3Preliminary Examination

Pinhole: improves VA → refractive (correctable); no improvement → organic disease. Far VA at 6 m, near at 40 cm. Binocular VA should be ≥ the better monocular eye.
Reflexes: corneal reflex temporal = eso, nasal = exo. Pupillary light reflex branches at the pretectal nucleus (before the LGN). Near triad = constriction + accommodation + convergence.
NPC norm break ~7 cm (>10 = CI, <5 = CE). Unilateral cover test distinguishes phoria vs tropia; alternating cover gives total deviation only. RAPD (Marcus Gunn) = swinging flashlight.
Headaches: Frontal = hyperopia/CE · Temporal = astigmatism · Occipital = presbyopia/CI.
Trap: a nasal corneal reflex means esotropia. No — temporal reflex = eso; nasal = exo.
Trap: the alternating cover test tells phoria from tropia. No — it shows total deviation; use the unilateral cover test to differentiate.

Higher condensing lens (e.g. +20 vs +14) = wider field, less magnification. Van Herick: grade 3–4 safe to dilate; grade 0–1 do not dilate.
Javal's rule: At = 1.25(Ac) + (−0.50 cyl × 90°); internal astigmatism ≈ −0.50 × 90° (ATR). Keratometer measures the anterior corneal surface only (n ≈ 1.3375).
Filters: cobalt blue = fluorescein staining; red-free (green) = blood vessels. Opacity parallax: anterior moves opposite the scope, posterior moves with it.
Trap: direct ophthalmoscopy gives an inverted image. No — direct is erect/virtual; indirect is inverted/real.
Trap: a stronger condensing lens magnifies more. No — higher power = wider field, LESS magnification.

C5Subjective Examination

Fog +0.50 to +0.75 D above best sphere (target 20/30–20/40). Astigmatic dial: darkest line = power meridian; minus-cyl axis is 90° from it. Add +0.25 D sphere per −0.50 D cyl.
JCC: prefer Position 1 (red dots ∥ axis) → cylinder under-powered → add minus. Prefer Position 2 → reduce minus. Rotation method for cyl > 0.75 D; JCC for ≤ 0.75 D.
Duochrome: Green clearer → Give plus / reduce minus (over-minused). Red clearer → add minus. Invalid in red-green color deficiency.
Binocular balance: re-fog +0.50–0.75 D to BOTH eyes; dissociate 3Δ BD OD / 3Δ BU OS; if +0.25 reverses preference, let the dominant eye keep better VA. PRA = add minus; NRA = add plus. OEP #7 endpoint = MPMVA.
Trap: the darkest astigmatic-dial line is the axis. No — it is the power meridian; the minus-cyl axis is 90° away.

C6Management of Visual Disorders

Management order: correct refractive error → prism → vision therapy → surgery.
Prism: exophoria → base in; esophoria → base out; hyperphoria → base down before the higher eye. Sheard's prism = (2 × phoria − recovery) ÷ 3.
Amblyopia: correct the Rx first; patch the GOOD eye (or atropine-penalize the good eye). Surgery aligns but does NOT treat amblyopia. Pleoptics is for eccentric fixation only. Critical period most sensitive 0–2 years.
Accommodative esotropia: full cycloplegic hyperopic correction first. Over-patching risks rebound amblyopia. Distinguish divergence insufficiency (comitant) from CN6 palsy (incomitant).
Trap: under-correct the amblyopic eye to avoid stress. No — it needs the clearest image; under-correction perpetuates suppression.
Trap: surgery cures amblyopia. No — it only corrects alignment; acuity still needs patching/penalization/VT.

C7Vision Therapy & Orthoptics

Glasses first, always. VT expands fusional capacity; prism only compensates. Sheard's/Percival's decide prism, not VT.
Parks 3-step (vertical deviations only): Higher eye → worse Gaze → worse Tilt, narrowing 8 → 4 → 2 → 1 candidate muscles.
Brock string: normal = X at the fixated bead; absent X = suppression (diagnostic AND therapeutic). CAM is monocular (occlude the dominant eye), ~7 min, weekly. Lens flippers ±2.00 D, target ~12 cpm.
CI office sequence: pencil push-ups → Brock string → aperture rule → prism/vectogram flippers. Part-time patching (2–6 hrs) ≈ full-time for moderate amblyopia (PEDIG); stop only at equalization or plateau.
Trap: CAM is done with both eyes open. No — it is monocular; the dominant eye is occluded.
Trap: Parks 3-step works for horizontal phorias. No — vertical (cyclovertical) deviations only.
